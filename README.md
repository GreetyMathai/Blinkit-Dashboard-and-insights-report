# Blinkit-Dashboard-and-insights-report
This project analyzes Blinkitâ€™s sales, outlet performance, and customer buying behavior using an interactive Power BI dashboard. The analysis focuses on understanding regional trends, outlet efficiency, and product preferences to support data-driven retail decisions.

## Objectives
- Compare sales across city tiers (Tier 1, Tier 2, Tier 3)
- Evaluate performance by outlet size and outlet type
- Identify top-performing product categories and fat content preferences
- Analyze outlet expansion trends over time

## Dataset Summary
Product-level data: Item category, fat content, visibility, rating, sales
Outlet-level data: Outlet size, outlet type, location tier, establishment year

## Charts Used
- KPI Cards: Display high-level business metrics such as Total Sales, Total Items Sold, Average Rating, and Average Sales per Item. These provide a quick snapshot of overall performance.

- Bar Charts: Used to compare sales across location tiers, outlet sizes, outlet types, and product categories. These charts help identify top-performing regions, store formats, and high-demand categories at a glance.

- Donut Charts: Represent proportional distributions such as Low-Fat vs Regular-Fat product preference and outlet type contribution to total sales, making it easy to understand market share and customer preferences.

- Stacked Column Charts: Show how multiple dimensions interact, such as outlet size performance across city tiers and category-wise sales within each tier, enabling deeper comparative analysis.

- Line Chart: Visualizes outlet establishment trends over time, highlighting expansion phases, peak growth years, and periods of saturation across tiers.

- Tables / Matrix Visuals: Provide detailed views of sales, ratings, and visibility metrics by outlet type, supporting granular analysis and validation of high-level trends.

## Key Insights Generated
- Tier 3 is the highest-performing and fastest-growing region.
- Low-Fat products consistently outperform Regular-Fat items.
- Supermarket Type 1 drives the highest sales due to better visibility and volume.
- Medium outlets are the most scalable and consistently performing format.
- Fruits & Vegetables, Snacks, and Household Items dominate sales across all tiers.

## Business Use Cases
- Expansion Strategy: Launch large outlets in Tier 1, scale medium outlets in Tier 2 and Tier 3, and prioritize continued growth in Tier 3

- Product Visibility & Assortment Optimization: Improve visibility in Supermarket Type 2 and 3 while strengthening essential and Low-Fat product ranges

- Tier-Specific Product Strategy: Tailor assortments with premium and healthy options in Tier 1, essentials in Tier 2, and a balanced mix in Tier 3

- Operational Optimization: Replicate Supermarket Type 1 best practices, improve Tier 3 restocking, and expand frozen and convenience sections

- Customer Experience & Targeted Promotions: Ensure product availability and run tier-specific promotions to boost satisfaction and loyalty

This dashboard transforms retail data into actionable insights that help optimize outlet strategy, product assortment, and regional expansion. It serves as a strong example of how Power BI can support strategic decision-making in retail analytics.
